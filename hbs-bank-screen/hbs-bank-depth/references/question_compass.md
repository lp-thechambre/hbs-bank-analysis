# Question Compass — Qualitative Analysis Guide

This document provides open-ended questions organized by HBS methodology chapter. It is a **compass, not a checklist** — guiding exploration rather than prescribing box-ticking. Each chapter has core concerns, red flag scenarios, and cross-chapter links.

---

## Ch3: Capital Adequacy

### Core Concerns
- Is the bank's capital position genuinely strong or cosmetically managed? Look beyond the headline CET1 ratio.
- How does the bank balance capital retention vs shareholder returns? What does the dividend policy reveal about management's capital philosophy?
- Are there off-balance-sheet exposures or risk-weighted asset optimizations that make the reported ratios look better than reality?

### Red Flag Scenarios
- CET1 ratio stable but RWA growth significantly below loan growth → RWA optimization may be masking risk
- High dividend payout with CET1 near regulatory minimum → prioritizing shareholders over safety

### Cross-Chapter Links
- Ch3 capital adequacy → Ch19 resilience: Did the bank maintain capital discipline during stress years?
- Ch3 capital policy → Ch17 capital planning: Is the capital plan credible given the growth ambitions?

---

## Ch5: Asset Quality

### Core Concerns
- What is the TRUE asset quality picture? Look at overdue loans, watch-list loans, and restructured loans — not just NPL ratio.
- Is NPL recognition conservative or aggressive? The overdue-to-NPL ratio tells you more than the NPL ratio itself.
- Where is the next NPL wave likely to come from? Concentration analysis by industry and region.

### Red Flag Scenarios
- NPL ratio declining while overdue loans increasing → recognition lag, not genuine improvement
- Provision coverage at exactly 120% every quarter → provisioning is managed, not risk-based
- Rapid loan growth in a single industry that's facing macro headwinds

### Cross-Chapter Links
- Ch5 asset quality → Ch20 integrity: NPL recognition aggressiveness is a key integrity indicator
- Ch5 asset quality → Ch18 concentration: Industry concentration amplifies asset quality risk

---

## Ch6: Profitability & Efficiency

### Core Concerns
- Is profitability sustainable or propped up by one-off items, reserve releases, or accounting choices?
- What's driving the PPOP trend? Decompose into volume, margin, and fee income components.
- How much of profit growth comes from genuine business expansion vs provision reduction?

### Red Flag Scenarios
- Net profit growing faster than PPOP for multiple periods → provision release fueling earnings
- Cost-income ratio declining but revenue per employee declining too → cutting costs but losing talent
- ROE maintained only through increasing leverage (equity/assets declining)

### Cross-Chapter Links
- Ch6 profitability → Ch9 NIM: Margin compression directly feeds into PPOP and profitability
- Ch6 efficiency → Ch11 operating efficiency: Cost-income decomposition reveals structural cost issues

---

## Ch7: Liquidity

### Core Concerns
- Is the funding structure stable or fragile? Look at deposit concentration, wholesale funding dependence, and LCR composition.
- Does the bank have genuine deposit franchise or is it buying deposits at high cost?
- What's the maturity mismatch between assets and liabilities?

### Red Flag Scenarios
- LCR consistently above regulatory minimum but HQLA heavily concentrated in a single asset type
- Retail deposit ratio declining while interbank borrowing increasing
- Deposit growth driven entirely by high-cost structured deposits

### Cross-Chapter Links
- Ch7 liquidity → Ch8 customer base: Deposit franchise strength depends on customer quality
- Ch7 liquidity → Ch15 market risk: Interest rate risk from maturity mismatch

---

## Ch8: Customer Base

### Core Concerns
- Does the bank earn its customers through brand/network/service, or does it buy them through rate/ promotion?
- Is customer quality improving or deteriorating? Use the marginal deposit contribution matrix.
- How sticky are customers? What's the deposit retention rate? The churn pattern?

### Red Flag Scenarios
- Customer count growing faster than deposit growth + marginal acquisition cost rising → buying low-quality customers
- Corporate customer count disclosure stops + corporate deposit growth declining → may be masking corporate business contraction
- Retail AUM growth entirely from market appreciation, not net new money inflows

### Cross-Chapter Links
- Ch8 customer quality → Ch9 NIM: Low-quality customers with rate-sensitive deposits compress NIM
- Ch8 customer acquisition → Ch6 cost-income: Rising acquisition costs hit efficiency
- Ch8 corporate customer decline → Ch18 concentration: May signal corporate loan book problems

---

## Ch9: Net Interest Margin (NIM)

### Core Concerns
- What's driving NIM changes? Decompose into asset yield changes vs funding cost changes.
- Is the bank's asset mix shifting toward higher-yield but riskier loans?
- How does the bank's NIM compare to peers with a similar loan/deposit mix?

### Red Flag Scenarios
- NIM stable but asset yield rising rapidly → reaching for yield, likely taking more credit risk
- NIM compression faster than peers → competitive position weakening or asset quality forcing de-risking
- Reported NIM significantly different from (interest_income - interest_expense) / average earning assets → non-standard calculation

### Cross-Chapter Links
- Ch9 NIM → Ch5 asset quality: Reaching for yield creates future NPLs
- Ch9 NIM → Ch8 customer base: Rate-sensitive customers drive funding cost volatility

---

## Ch10: Fee Income

### Core Concerns
- Is fee income diversified or concentrated in a few volatile categories?
- What's the quality of fee income? Recurring (custody, wealth management AUM fees) vs transactional (bancassurance one-offs)?
- Does fee income growth come from genuine business expansion or fee rate increases?

### Red Flag Scenarios
- Fee income growth driven by a single product category that's under regulatory scrutiny
- Q4 fee income spike significantly above quarterly average → year-end revenue pulling
- Fee income growing but related customer activity metrics (transaction volume, AUM) not growing

### Cross-Chapter Links
- Ch10 fee income → Ch20 integrity: Q4 fee income concentration is a revenue timing red flag
- Ch10 fee income → Ch8 customer base: Wealth management fees depend on customer quality

---

## Ch11: Operating Efficiency

### Core Concerns
- Is the cost structure aligned with the bank's strategy? A retail-focused bank should have higher opex but better deposit franchise.
- Is the bank investing enough in technology? Under-investment in IT today becomes competitive disadvantage tomorrow.
- What's happening to headcount and per-employee metrics?

### Red Flag Scenarios
- Cost-income ratio declining but IT/innovation capex also declining → cutting muscle, not fat
- Branch count declining while digital adoption metrics not disclosed → may be losing customers during branch rationalization
- Revenue per employee declining → productivity problem

### Cross-Chapter Links
- Ch11 efficiency → Ch22 strategy: Cost structure must align with strategic positioning
- Ch11 efficiency → Ch6 profitability: Efficiency is the denominator of sustainable profitability

---

## Ch12: Growth

### Core Concerns
- Is growth quality-adjusted? A bank growing loans 20% into a deteriorating sector is destroying value.
- What's driving growth? Market share gains (positive) vs credit standard loosening (negative)?
- Is growth funded by stable deposits or wholesale borrowing?

### Red Flag Scenarios
- Loan growth far exceeding deposit growth → funding gap widening, eventual liquidity pressure
- Growth concentrated in a single region or industry that's showing stress
- Revenue growth negative despite loan growth positive → lending at ever-thinner margins

### Cross-Chapter Links
- Ch12 growth → Ch5 asset quality: Fast growth today = NPLs tomorrow if underwriting standards are loose
- Ch12 growth → Ch7 liquidity: Loan growth without deposit growth = funding stress

---

## Ch13: Risk Appetite

### Core Concerns
- Does the bank have a clearly articulated risk appetite and does actual behavior match it?
- Are risk limits set at meaningful levels or so high they never bind?
- What happens when a risk limit is breached? Is there evidence of consequences?

### Red Flag Scenarios
- Risk appetite statement unchanged for 3+ years while business mix has shifted significantly
- Risk limits consistently at 80-90% utilization for all categories → limits are too loose
- No evidence of limit breach consequences in board minutes

---

## Ch14: Credit Risk

### Core Concerns
- How does the bank underwrite? Is it rules-based, model-based, or relationship-based?
- What are the early warning indicators the bank monitors, and are they leading or lagging?
- How does the bank handle a deteriorating borrower? Forbearance, restructuring, or recognition?

### Red Flag Scenarios
- Restructured loans growing faster than NPLs → evergreening, not resolving
- Collateral valuation policies not updated for market price declines
- Related-party lending growing as a share of total loans

---

## Ch15: Market Risk

### Core Concerns
- What's the duration gap? Is the bank positioned for rising or falling rates?
- How much of the investment portfolio is in mark-to-market vs hold-to-maturity?
- Are there concentrated trading positions or derivatives exposures?

### Red Flag Scenarios
- HTM portfolio >> AFS portfolio → hiding mark-to-market losses
- Duration gap significantly different from peers → directional rate bet
- Derivative notional amounts large relative to capital

---

## Ch16: Operational Risk

### Core Concerns
- What are the major operational risk events in the past 3 years?
- Is there a pattern of recurring issues suggesting systemic control weakness?
- How much is spent on risk controls vs how much is lost to operational risk events?

### Red Flag Scenarios
- Repeated same-category operational risk losses → control environment failure
- IT system incidents increasing in frequency or severity
- Key person risk: critical functions concentrated in few individuals

---

## Ch17: Capital Planning

### Core Concerns
- Is the capital plan internally consistent? Do growth assumptions match capital generation capacity?
- What's the contingency plan if capital falls below target?
- Does the capital plan consider stress scenarios beyond regulatory minimums?

### Red Flag Scenarios
- Capital plan assumes continued profit growth at historical rates despite margin compression
- No external capital raising capacity (low stock price, constrained major shareholders)
- Dividend policy not aligned with capital plan projections

---

## Ch18: Concentration Risk

### Core Concerns
- Where is the bank's loan book concentrated? By industry, region, and single-name exposure.
- Are there hidden concentrations (e.g., loans to local government platforms classified across multiple industries)?
- How does the top-10 borrower exposure compare to capital?

### Red Flag Scenarios
- Top-3 industry exposures > 50% of corporate loans
- Real estate + construction + related sectors > 30% of total loans
- Single-name exposure to any borrower > 10% of CET1

## Ch19: Resilience

### Core Concerns
- How did management behave during the last crisis? This is the single best predictor of future behavior.
- Did they protect the balance sheet or protect the stock price?
- Did they invest through the cycle or cut and run?

Reference `references/voh_framework.md` — resilience is scored per-bank by Vice (L5a). L3 feeds management assessment signals; Vice assigns final score.

## Ch20: Integrity

### Core Concerns
- Do the numbers tell a consistent story across financial statements, regulatory filings, and Pillar 3?
- Is there evidence of earnings management, provisioning games, or disclosure games?
- Does management's public narrative match the data?

Reference `references/voh_framework.md` — integrity is scored per-bank by Vice (L5a). L3 feeds integrity_red_flags from qualitative analysis; Vice assigns final score.

---

## Ch21: Management Quality

### Core Concerns
- What's the management team's track record? Have they delivered on past strategic promises?
- Is there stability or churn in key positions? CFO departure without clear reason is a red flag.
- How aligned are management incentives with long-term value creation?

### Red Flag Scenarios
- Key executives departing shortly after negative earnings surprise
- Compensation structure rewards short-term profit growth with no clawback provisions
- Board lacks independent directors with banking expertise

---

## Ch22: Strategy

### Core Concerns
- Is the bank's strategy coherent? Do the pieces (branch network, technology, talent, capital) fit together?
- Has the strategy changed significantly from 3 years ago? Pivot without clear rationale = lack of direction.
- Are competitors executing the same strategy better?

### Red Flag Scenarios
- Strategy described in vague buzzwords without specific resource allocation commitments
- Major strategy pivot without board/management changes → same team, new plan = low credibility
- Strategy inconsistent with capital allocation (e.g., "retail transformation" but 80% of loans still corporate)

---

## Ch23: ESG & Governance

### Core Concerns
- Is governance genuine or cosmetic? Independent directors with real power or rubber stamps?
- What are the bank's environmental risk exposures? Not just green finance — what's the carbon footprint of the loan book?
- Are there social risks (labor practices, data privacy, financial inclusion compliance)?

### Red Flag Scenarios
- Board controlled by a single major shareholder with related-party loan history
- Green finance labeled loans growing rapidly but without third-party verification
- Female board representation < 10% with no plan to improve
